The vulnerability occurs because `DestroyImage` frees the `Image` object and returns `NULL`, but the caller in `WriteMSLImage` uses `msl_image=DestroyImage(msl_image);` immediately after `ProcessMSLScript`, which may have stored a pointer to `msl_image` in `msl_info.image` and later freed it again via `DestroyMSLInfo`'s loop, leading to a double-free that could cause use-after-free.
